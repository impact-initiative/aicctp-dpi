About the Conference

Context and Purpose

This conference is convened as the culminating feedback event for a joint research project titled "A Paradigmatic Shift in Public Service Delivery: Accessible, Inclusive, and Secure Digital Public Infrastructure", implemented by the Indian Council for Research on International Economic Relations (ICRIER) and the University of Queensland (UQ Cyber).

The project is funded by the Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT), Australia, under the Australia-India Cyber and Critical Technology Partnership (AICCTP). It examines Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) as a paradigmatic shift in public service delivery, generating policy-relevant evidence on how DPI can be designed and governed to deliver accessible, inclusive, and secure public services.

The research is structured around two focal use cases: India's Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) ecosystem and healthcare provisioning through the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM), drawing broader lessons for the Indo-Pacific region. The conference will present preliminary findings from both case studies and seek structured expert feedback ahead of the final report.

Analytical Framework

Three Research Pillars

The project evaluates DPI-enabled public service delivery along three interconnected analytical dimensions.

01
Accessibility and Inclusivity

How DPI systems can be designed to ensure equitable access and last-mile delivery across diverse populations, with particular attention to vulnerable and marginalised groups.

02
Efficiency

Assessment of administrative efficiency, fund flow integration, interoperability, and monitoring within large-scale DPI-enabled welfare and health architectures.

03
Security

Examination of cybersecurity risks, data governance frameworks, privacy-preserving architectures, and institutional safeguards within DPI-enabled public service delivery systems.

Project Background

Panel Workshops

Two panel workshops were conducted as part of the primary research programme, engaging policymakers, technologists, researchers, civil society, and practitioners on each focal use case.

Workshop 1 of 2
Transformation of Health Systems through Digital Public Infrastructure
9 January 2026 India Habitat Centre, New Delhi 42 Participants Hybrid

A full-day hybrid workshop examining India's Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM) as a focal DPI health case. Experts from government, the private sector, international organisations, and civil society deliberated on technical foundations, governance, public-private partnerships, and cybersecurity in DPI-enabled health systems.

Outcome Document
Workshop 2 of 2
Digital Public Infrastructure and Public Service Delivery: Inclusion. Efficiency. Security.
8 May 2026 India Habitat Centre, New Delhi Roundtable

A roundtable bringing together policymakers, technologists, civil society, and cybersecurity experts to deliberate on India's Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) ecosystem. Discussions examined the interaction between inclusion, efficiency, and security in DPI-enabled welfare delivery systems.
